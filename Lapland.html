Where to go? Christmas?

Lapland is the largest and northernmost region of Finland. Lapland's cold and wintry climate, coupled with the relative abundance of conifer trees such as pines and spruces means that it has become associated with Christmas in some countries.

THE MAGICAL, MYTHICAL LAPLAND

Finnish Lapland is as close as reality gets to those who dream of a winter wonderland. Contrasts are a key factor in the allure of Lapland where 24-hour sunlight in the summer replaces the dark winter days. The hustle and bustle of towns and ski resorts is just minutes away from the peace and quiet of the wild wilderness.

SANTA CLAUS

Everyone knows the one and only Santa Claus is Finnish and lives in Lapland. However, as the exact location of his hideaway is not known, it is best to head to his Rovaniemi office. This is where he greets visitors all year round.

The midnight sun, Summer

May – June – July

During the season surrounding the summer solstice in Rovaniemi, the sun gives us its radiant face 24 hours a day and for several weeks. Even during the month of August, the "night" consists of a long period of twilight rather than true darkness.

Late Summer- Autumn

August-October

This is a season full of colors and Northern Lights, while summer activities are still available, the days get shorter. This is the best season for hiking.

The Polar Night "Kaamos"

November- February

From November to February, Lapland is distinguished by painting the most relaxing shades of blue and only the dimmer light covers the ground, while the region prepares for its winter dream. During this period known as The Polar Night "Kaamos", the sun does not rise above the horizon. The brightness of the stars and the light reflected by the moon, combined with the whitest and purest snow, illuminate the day and more than compensate for the lack of sunlight on clear days. The sleepy world of snow and ice finds its equilibrium when the scintillation of the Northern Lights crosses the clear skies of winter nights. This phenomenon is visible anywhere in Lapland - if the weather conditions are adequate, the best months for hunting Auroras are February - March and September - October. This is a good season for all kinds of winter activities and for the hunting of Auroras. The atmosphere is absolutely ideal to spend New Year and Christmas.

The arrival of Spring – Sunny Winter

This is the best season for winter activities and long Safaris. It is also the best time to observe the Northern Lights as the days become longer and warmer.
