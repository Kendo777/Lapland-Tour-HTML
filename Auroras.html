AURORAS

A miracle of nature

What is an Aurora? I want photos now!

Auroras are the result of disturbances in the magnetosphere caused by solar wind. These disturbances are sometimes strong enough to alter the trajectories of charged particles in both solar wind and magnetospheric plasma.

Where can I see it? The legend of the Aurora

The Northern Lights appear 200 nights a year in Finnish Lapland. The further north you go, the more likely you are to see it. Saariselk, Kilpisjorvi and Inari, along with the rest of Lapland, are recommended places in Finland. In Helsinki and in the south, the Northern Lights can be seen about twenty nights a year, away from urban lights.

A Sami legend tells that the auroras happen when a fox crosses the Arctic plateaus and illuminates the sky with the sparks that come out of its tail as the snow swirls.
